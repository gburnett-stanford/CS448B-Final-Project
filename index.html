If you're reading this, chances are you've been injured at least once during your lifetime. Over the course of a lifetime, the everyday objects that can harm us change as we age. The Consumer Product Safety Commission, or CPSC, is an organization within the United States whose main mission is to protect the public from unreasonable risks of injury or death caused by consumer products. Americans who have been injured or died as well as property damage caused from consumer products cost the US more than more than $1 trillion every year.

This project utilizes the CSPC's data on injuries and deaths caused by consumer products allowing us to take a look at 20,674 Americans who were injured by consumer products in 2020. Let's first take a look at high chairs.

High Chairs

The demographic most at risk with High Chairs are infants under the age of 1.

Understanding that this is the most dangerous consumer product to those < 1 years old is just one part of the bigger picture. The other part is utilizing that data in order to reduce the amount of injuries and deaths occurring to better protect the users of the products.

Why this Data Helps

Having this knowledge of which parts of the high chair are the most dangerous allows the commission to narrow down the root cause of these hazard patterns and what needs to be changed. For example, with the case of restraint systems, the CPSC found that a more stringent standard for labels and instructional literature than the existing standard (ASTM F404-15) would further reduce the risk of injuries within this hazard pattern.

If you are reading this article, it is likely you are not under the age of 1, however, this idea of identifying hazard patterns can be applied to all consumer products to make them safer and in turn result in less injuries or even deaths for everyone in the US.
